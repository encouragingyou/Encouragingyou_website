Safeguarding

Separate child and adult safeguarding routes, explained plainly.

If you are worried about someone's safety or wellbeing, use the correct route as soon as possible. If someone is in immediate danger, call 999.

For launch, safeguarding concerns can be sent to admin@encouragingyou.co.uk with the subject line “Safeguarding concern”.

Child safeguarding

Use this route when a child or young person may be at risk of harm, abuse, neglect, exploitation, or unsafe treatment.

  • Share what you saw, heard, or were told as soon as possible.
  • Do not promise confidentiality that you cannot keep.
  • Record key facts clearly and pass them on promptly.

Adult safeguarding

Use this route when an adult with care and support needs may be experiencing abuse, neglect, coercion, or unsafe circumstances.

  • Listen carefully and take concerns seriously.
  • Share only what is necessary with the right safeguarding contact.
  • Act quickly if there is immediate risk.
