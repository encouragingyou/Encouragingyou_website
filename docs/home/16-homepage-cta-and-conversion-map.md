# Homepage CTA And Conversion Map

## CTA tiers

Primary:
- `Join a session`
  - Main audience: young people, parents/carers, referrers
  - Role: route visitors who are ready to inspect live session detail

Secondary:
- `Get support`
  - Main audience: young people, parents/carers, referrers, community members
  - Role: low-friction fallback when someone is unsure which route fits

Supporting:
- `Get CV help`
  - Role: practical route for the most immediate support need after the primary CTA pair
- `See all programmes`
  - Role: broaden understanding for visitors who need the bigger picture first
- `Volunteer or partner`
  - Role: route supporters into `Get involved` without competing with support-first actions

Trust-supporting:
- `Why EncouragingYou exists`
- `Safeguarding information`
- `Privacy notice`

## Route relationships

- `Sessions`: the highest-priority destination for ready visitors.
- `Contact`: the low-friction fallback when certainty is low.
- `Programmes`: the explanation layer for wider support understanding.
- `Get involved`: the supporter/referrer hub; it should not overpower support seekers.
- `Safeguarding`: trust-critical and always visible, but not treated as a primary conversion CTA.

## Quick-action rail

The quick-action rail is no longer borrowed from navigation config. It now belongs to the homepage strategy itself because it is part of route-level conversion logic, not global shell structure.

Quick actions at launch:

1. `Join a session`
2. `Get CV help`
3. `Get support`
4. `Volunteer or partner`

## Audience-specific journeys

- Young people: `Join a session` first, `Get support` second, `Get CV help` as the most specific supporting route.
- Parents/carers: `Join a session` first, `Get support` second, with safeguarding/privacy/programme context nearby.
- Referrers: `Join a session` or `Get support`, with programme context for fit-checking.
- Community members: `Get support` first, `See community support` second.
- Volunteers: `Volunteer or partner` first, `Email the team` second.
- Partners: `Volunteer or partner` first, `Email the team` second, with about/community context for credibility.

## Launch exclusions

The homepage intentionally does not introduce:

- invented proof or impact numbers
- forced newsletter-style engagement
- partner/funder asks that outrank support-seeking routes
- generic `Learn more` CTAs where a clearer route already exists
