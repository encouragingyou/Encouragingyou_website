# Prompt 49 - Editorial Polish and Voice QA

## What Changed

| Route family | Editorial action | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Home | Tightened first-step, trust-strip, wider-community, and get-involved copy. | The homepage reads like a finished public route instead of a planning document. |
| About | Kept the origin, mission, vision, and youth-led story rooted in the approved brief. | The page now reads as a clear organisational introduction with less editorial scaffolding. |
| Programmes | Reworked programme-family copy to remove internal “this page should…” phrasing. | Each pillar now explains the offer more directly while keeping proof boundaries visible. |
| Sessions | Rewrote reassurance and expectation copy so it sounds supportive and practical, not like QA notes. | Session pages now carry calmer, more user-facing first-visit language. |
| Contact / Get involved | Tightened route-choice, channel, and next-step language. | Public routes are clearer without losing privacy and safeguarding boundaries. |
| Volunteer / Partner | Removed planning tone from key support, process, and trust panels. | These routes now sound more credible and less like internal guidance. |
| FAQs | Replaced governance-heavy answers with direct public wording. | Answers are clearer, shorter, and closer to real visitor questions. |

## Remaining Deliberate Boundaries

- Public-proof notices still appear where they help explain why named proof, venue detail, or photography is absent.
- The site continues to prefer “ask first” language over guessing price, referral, or access rules.
- Legal and safeguarding pages remain precise rather than overly warm where precision matters more.

## QA Notes For Prompt 50

- Confirm that none of the remaining proof-boundary notices feel repetitive after the Prompt 49 copy tightening.
