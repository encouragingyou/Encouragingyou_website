# Prompt 30 - Role Pathway And Screening Model

Prompt 30 formalizes what the public volunteer route can say now and what must remain contact-led.

## Publicly supported pathway truth

- Session support and welcome
  - credible public shape: helping create a calm, welcoming environment around sessions or community activity
  - launch rule: describe the pathway, not a fixed rota or exact task list
- Skills and encouragement
  - credible public shape: bringing practical skills, encouragement, or lived understanding that supports confidence-building work
  - launch rule: keep the fit conversation explicit before any role is implied
- Occasional practical help
  - credible public shape: lighter-touch, one-off, or community-linked help when regular volunteering is not realistic
  - launch rule: keep this distinct from formal partnership or supporter routes

## Support and induction rules

- The first step is a conversation, not a long-form application.
- Public copy can promise explanation of fit, boundaries, and expectations before anything starts.
- Public copy must not promise accredited training, fixed staffing models, or named induction stages that the repo does not verify.

## Safeguarding and checks rules

- Safeguarding stays visible before the enquiry path, not after it.
- The route can say that checks depend on the responsibility involved.
- The route can use the verified public statement that EncouragingYou follows safeguarding procedures and uses trained, vetted staff and volunteers.
- The route must not claim that every volunteer pathway uses the same check level or the same screening workflow.

## Time-commitment rules

- Encourage honest availability over bigger promises.
- Keep occasional and regular help distinct.
- Leave exact frequency or shift commitments to the first conversation unless the repo later verifies them.
