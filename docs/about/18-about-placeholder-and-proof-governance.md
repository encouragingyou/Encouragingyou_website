# About Placeholder And Proof Governance

The About route is trust-critical, so Prompt 18 treats missing proof as a design problem rather than something to hide.

## Public proof strategy

The route is allowed to publish:

- the origin story and founder motivation from the approved brief
- the youth-led posture and lived-experience framing from the approved brief
- mission and vision summaries from canonical site settings
- trust signals that already have a source-backed public basis
- clear routes into Programmes, Sessions, Contact, Get involved, Privacy, and Safeguarding

The route is not allowed to invent:

- impact statistics
- testimonials
- named leadership biographies
- team portraits
- partner logos or partner claims without approval
- operational claims that still depend on confirmation, such as trained-and-vetted wording

## Route behavior

The proof boundary is now visible in-page via `proofBoundary`, using three public buckets:

1. safe to publish now
2. still waiting for approval
3. held back until verified

This keeps the page feeling complete without pretending the organisation has already published every proof surface it may eventually want.

## Editorial rule

The route follows the existing Prompt 15 editorial contract for template `about`:

- explain credibility, purpose, and youth-led posture without sounding self-congratulatory
- keep local rootedness and practical care visible
- keep unverified leadership or impact claims out of the page

Prompt 18 turns that from a docs-only rule into a renderable production surface.
