# Prompt 34 - Child Adult Boundary And Escalation Model

## Boundary split

The public site now treats child and adult safeguarding as separate high-trust routes.

### Child safeguarding

Use when the concern is about a child or young person under 18, including:

- abuse
- neglect
- exploitation or grooming
- unsafe adult behaviour
- a serious disclosure or welfare concern

### Adult safeguarding

Use when the concern is about an adult with care and support needs, including:

- abuse
- neglect
- coercion or control
- exploitation
- unsafe circumstances where care/support needs make the risk more serious

## Escalation order

The route family now keeps the same public order everywhere:

1. If there is immediate danger or urgent emergency need, call `999` first.
2. If the concern is serious but not an emergency, use the dedicated safeguarding route.
3. Share the facts plainly and proportionately.
4. Do not use ordinary contact as a substitute for safeguarding reporting.

## Shared public rules

- do not promise confidentiality that the organisation has not defined publicly
- do not wait for a perfect account before raising a serious concern
- do not ask users to choose between complicated internal thresholds
- do keep the child/adult distinction visible so the page is easier to trust and easier to scan

## Detail-route composition

Each branch route now contains:

- the emergency boundary
- a route-summary panel
- route-specific step-by-step escalation guidance
- a preparation checklist
- a limits/what-this-page-does-not-promise section
- the shared secure safeguarding form
- the shared public-contact, policy, and proof-boundary panels

## Cross-link rule

Each branch route links to the other safeguarding branch, but the instruction is:

- use the closest route and explain what you know
- do not delay reporting just because the perfect label is unclear
